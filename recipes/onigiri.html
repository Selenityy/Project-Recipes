Onigiri

My Favorite go to lunch prep!

Onigiri are japanese for rice balls. They can include any kind of filling, the most popular being tuna, salmon and red bean. The recipe on this site will be creating spicy tuna onigiri!

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Tuna
  • Japanese Mayo
  • Siriacha
  • Rice Seasoning
  • Salt
  • Seaweed

Culinary tools you will need:

  • Rice Cooker
  • Onigiri mold optional

Steps

  1. Take 2 cups of rice and place them in the rice cooker bowl. Make sure to wash the rice a few times to ensure it's clean.
  2. Dry off the rice cooker bowl and place it back in the cooker itself. Set the rice to Quick Cook.
  3. Open your can of tuna, and drain the water.
  4. Mix in a 1:1 ratio of mayo and siracha to the tuna. Stir and taste. Repeat this step as many times as needed until you like the tuna taste.
  5. Take your seaweed sheets and cut them into equally 1 inch thick strips.
  6. Lay out the rice seasoning in a line, we wll be rolling the completed onigri through this for the edges to be seasoned.
  7. Once the rice is cooked, let it cool off a bit as to not burn your hands.
  8. From here, you'll want to grab a small amount and put it into your onigiri mold or in your wet hands to avoid rice sticking.
  9. Scoop a small amount of your spicy tuna creation and place on top of the rice. Make sure to not overload the rice or it will spill out the sides.
  10. Grab some more rice and place on top of the tuna.
  11. If you are using a mold, press down on the rice to make the triangle shape. If you are using your hands, press the rice together and use your fingers to pinch into a triangle shape. Make sure to really press and pack the rice so it doesn't come apart!
  12. Take the rice out of the mold and place a seaweed strip centered on the onigiri, wrapping the front and the back.
  13. Lastly, roll all the edges of the onigiri in rice seasoning.
  14. Enjoy while it's warm!
